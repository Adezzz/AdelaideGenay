About me

I was born in France, but spent most of my childhood in China where I lived for 9 years. I then spent a part of my teenagehood in Brazil (2 years) before returning to France when I was 16 years old.

Since then, I've been moving from town to town to pursue my studies in computer science. I graduated in 2019 from the French national engineering school ENSEIRB-MATMECA (Bordeaux), receiving my computer science degree specialized in multimedia and video game technologies.

After that, I conducted a Ph.D. thesis Inria in Bordeaux, directed by Martin Hachet and Anatole Lécuyer. My defense was the 13th of December, 2023. My research interests include avatars, augmented reality, and virtual embodiment. I talk about my work in several pop-science projects (in French): Three Minutes Thesis, Thèse et Vous ! podcast, The Conversation article, etc.
